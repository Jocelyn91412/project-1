K-Drama

Most Popular K-drama Globally

  1. Agent Kim Reactivated

    Manager Kim appears to be an unremarkable office worker and devoted single father, living a quiet life far removed from the world of espionage. Beneath that ordinary routine, however, lies a carefully hidden past: he was once an elite black-ops agent whose skills made him both invaluable and dangerous. When his daughter suddenly disappears, he has no choice but to abandon the anonymity he has worked so hard to build and return to the life he left behind.

    As his search intensifies, Kim finds himself pursued by powerful enemies while confronting the consequences of his secret identity. Forced to rely on instincts, training, and a handful of unexpected allies, he navigates a high-stakes world of covert operations, deception, and relentless action. Balancing fast-paced spy thrills with an emotional story about family and sacrifice, Agent Kim Reactivated delivers a character-driven adventure without losing sight of the personal stakes at its heart.

  2. Perfect Crown

    In an alternate 21st-century South Korea where the nation remains a constitutional monarchy, Seong Hui-ju appears to have everything—wealth, intelligence, and influence as the heir to one of the country's most powerful conglomerates. Yet despite her success, her status as an illegitimate daughter prevents her from ever being fully accepted within society's rigid hierarchy. Determined to take control of her future, she proposes an unexpected contract marriage to Grand Prince I-an, a royal whose prestigious title masks a life constrained by duty and a lack of real power.

    What begins as a practical arrangement soon places the pair at the center of palace politics, public scrutiny, and the competing ambitions of those who would rather see them fail. As they navigate the challenges of royal expectations and social prejudice, their partnership begins to test the boundaries between obligation and genuine connection. Blending romance, humor, and political intrigue, Perfect Crown explores themes of class, identity, and the courage to challenge long-standing traditions—all while remaining a heartfelt, spoiler-free modern fairy tale.

  3. The Judge Returns

    Lee Han-young is a respected judge whose career has become entangled with corruption after years of serving the interests of a powerful law firm. When a shocking turn of events gives him an extraordinary second chance, he awakens ten years in the past with full knowledge of the choices that led to his downfall. Armed with hindsight and a renewed sense of purpose, he sets out to rewrite his future and reclaim the principles he once abandoned.

    As Han-young navigates the legal system from a fresh starting point, he must outmaneuver influential figures who manipulate justice from the shadows while confronting the consequences of his former actions. Balancing courtroom drama, political intrigue, and elements of fantasy, The Judge Returns explores redemption, accountability, and the power of everyday choices. Rather than focusing solely on revenge, the series follows one man's determined effort to become the kind of judge—and person—he always should have been.
